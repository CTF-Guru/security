01
What CIEM is — least privilege for cloud identities

Cloud Infrastructure Entitlement Management discovers every identity in your cloud, computes what each can actually do, and drives it toward the minimum permission it truly needs.

Discover

Every identity — human and machine — across AWS · Azure · GCP.

Compute

The effective permission each one resolves to through layered policy.

Right-size

Trim the excess & unused; enforce least privilege continuously.

In the cloud, identity is the perimeter — CIEM is how you govern it.

02
The effective-permissions problem — why cloud IAM is genuinely hard

No human reads this correctly. Allows, inherited roles, resource grants and SCP denies interact — CIEM computes the real answer.

03
Why it exists
🌊
IAM is enormous — tens of thousands of possible permissions across each cloud.
📈
Over-permissioning is the norm — grants pile up; almost nobody trims them back.
🤖
Machines outnumber humans — service & workload identities now dominate the count.

Excess privilege is the blast-radius multiplier in every cloud breach.

04
What CIEM finds
👑
Excessive & unused — granted permissions never actually exercised.
🪜
Escalation paths — a low grant that can quietly bootstrap to admin.
🌉
Risky cross-account — identities that reach into accounts they shouldn't.
☣️
Toxic combinations — permission pairs that together enable harm.
05
Right-sizing to least privilege — from tangled grants to just-enough

Granted − used = the excess to cut. The trimmed role is enforced and re-checked as usage drifts.

06
Human vs machine — identities

👤 Human

Users & admins. Fewer, but interactive — MFA, sessions, login anomalies apply.

🤖 Machine

Service accounts, roles, workloads, keys. Vastly more numerous — and easy to over-grant & forget.

Non-human identities now dominate the cloud — and are where entitlement sprawl hides.

09
How it ties in
🛡️
Zero Trust — least privilege is a core tenet; CIEM measures & enforces it in cloud.
🗝️
PAM — governs privileged access; CIEM finds which entitlements are privileged.
⏱️
JIT — replaces standing grants with on-demand ones; CIEM tells you what to move.

CIEM maps the entitlement landscape; PAM/JIT change how power is granted over it.

10
Watch out
🧮
Effective ≠ granted — resolving policy + SCP + resource + identity is hard; trust the computed set.
🤖
Machine sprawl — non-human identities dominate & are the easiest to over-grant.
🧱
Remediate carefully — cut a live permission and you can break the app. Stage it.
⏳
Standing vs JIT — trimming helps; moving to just-in-time helps more.
11
The takeaway
CIEM answers "who can do what in my cloud — and who actually needs to?" — it computes effective permissions and drives every identity to least privilege.
Discover identities→ Compute effective→ Find the excess→ Right-size & enforce
