01
What SOC 2 is — an attestation report, not a certificate

SOC 2 = System and Organization Controls 2, an AICPA framework. A CPA firm attests to how well your controls meet the criteria — you get a report you can hand to customers, not a badge you display.

02
The 5 Trust Services Criteria

Security is mandatory — the other four are optional, scoped in only if they fit your service.

03
Scope in what fits

You choose which criteria the report covers — Security anchors every SOC 2, add the rest by relevance:

Security ★AvailabilityProcessing IntegrityConfidentialityPrivacy

A data pipeline may add Processing Integrity; a health app, Privacy. Scope to your promises.

04
Type I vs Type II — point in time vs over a period
Type IType II
QuestionAre controls designed suitably?Are they designed and operating effectively?
Whena single point in timeacross a review period (usually 3–12 months)
Evidencedesign of controls on that dateauditor samples evidence across the window
Buyersa fast first stepwhat customers usually demand

Type I proves the controls exist; Type II proves they actually ran, day after day.

05
The Type II observation period — sampled across the window

Type I is a snapshot; Type II is the whole movie — the auditor watches the controls run and tests samples along the way.

07
Who needs it & why
🏢
US SaaS & vendors — the assurance buyers ask for in due diligence.
🤝
Unblocks deals — procurement & security review clear faster with a report on file.
🔒
Shared under NDA — given to customers & prospects, not published.
08
The road to a report — define once, then keep it living
scope + pick TSC→ readiness / gap assessment→ implement controls→ audit: Type I→ Type II over a period→ annual renewal

A report is a point in a cycle — the observation window resets and you re-attest every year.

09
SOC 2 vs ISO 27001
SOC 2ISO 27001
US attestation reportglobal certification
criteria-based (TSC)a management system (ISMS)
CPA firm attestsaccredited body certifies

Not rivals — many vendors hold both; they satisfy different buyers.

10
Watch out
🚫
SOC 2 ≠ secure — it attests to your controls as scoped, not that you can't be breached.
🎯
Scope gaming — a narrow boundary or Security-only scope proves little. Read what's covered.
📅
Type I ≠ Type II — a point-in-time report says nothing about how controls held up over time.
📖
Read the exceptions — check the auditor's opinion & noted deviations, not just the cover.
11
The takeaway
SOC 2 is an independent CPA attestation to your controls against the Trust Services Criteria — a report you share, not a certificate you post.
Scope + TSC→ Operate controls→ Type II over a period→ Report under NDA
