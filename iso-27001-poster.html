01
What it is

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 is the international standard for an Information Security Management System (ISMS) — and it's certifiable. An accredited body audits your organization and issues a certificate the world recognizes.

It certifies how you manage security — a whole system, not one product or scan.

02
What an “ISMS” is

A management system to establish, operate, maintain, and continually improve how the organization protects information:

policiesrolesrisk processcontrols

Owned by leadership and run as a repeatable cycle — not a one-off audit or a shelf of documents.

03
The management-system clauses — 4 to 10, what you're audited against
🗺️
4 · Context — scope of the ISMS, interested parties, boundaries.
🧭
5 · Leadership — top management owns it; set the security policy.
⚠️
6 · Planning — risk assessment + treatment; the SoA.
🧰
7 · Support — people, competence, awareness, documented info.
🛠️
8 · Operation — run the risk process & controls day to day.
📊
9 · Performance — monitor, internal audit, management review.
🔁
10 · Improvement — fix nonconformities; keep raising the bar.
📌
Clauses 4–10 are mandatory — Annex A controls are chosen by risk.
04
Annex A — 93 controls, 4 themes — down from 114 / 14 domains in the 2013 edition

New & updated in 2022: threat intelligence, information security for cloud services, data leakage prevention, secure coding, and ICT readiness for business continuity. The 2013→2022 transition closed on 31 Oct 2025.

05
The risk-based flow — controls follow from risk, and the SoA records the choice

Core artifacts: the scope, the risk assessment & treatment plan, and the Statement of Applicability.

06
Plan-Do-Check-Act

The continual-improvement loop under every clause — it never stops turning.

07
27001 vs 27002
📜
ISO/IEC 27001 — the requirements. Certifiable. Annex A names the controls.
📖
ISO/IEC 27002 — implementation guidance for those controls. A companion — not certifiable.

You certify against 27001; you read 27002 to learn how to build each control. The 27002 controls map one-to-one to Annex A.

08
The certification journey — an accredited body audits, then a 3-year cycle
Stage 1 · readiness→ Stage 2 · implementation→ Certificate (3 yr)→ Surveillance (annual)→ Recertify

Stage 1 checks your documentation & readiness; Stage 2 checks the ISMS is actually implemented. The certificate lasts three years with annual surveillance audits, then recertification.

09
SOC 2 vs ISO 27001 — an attestation report vs a management-system certification

Both prove security maturity — SOC 2 reports on controls over a period; ISO 27001 certifies the system that runs them.

10
Watch out
🛡️
Certificate ≠ secure — it proves the system exists, not that you can't be breached.
🎯
Scope gaming — a narrow scope certifies little; check what the certificate covers.
📝
SoA exclusions — excluding a control is fine only if the risk justifies it.
🔁
Let the loop stall — skip surveillance or improvement and the certificate lapses.
11
The takeaway
ISO/IEC 27001 is the certifiable backbone for information security — clauses 4–10 run on Plan-Do-Check-Act, and Annex A controls are chosen by risk.
Scope the ISMS→ Assess & treat risk→ Statement of Applicability→ Audit · certify · improve
