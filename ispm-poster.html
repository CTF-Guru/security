01
What ISPM is — posture management for the identity fabric

Identity Security Posture Management — continuously assesses and hardens the posture of your identity providers and directories: Microsoft Entra ID (Azure AD), Okta, Ping, and on-prem Active Directory.

It takes the CSPM idea — inventory, find misconfigs & risky exposure, prioritize, remediate — and points it at identity. It answers two questions: is my identity infrastructure configured securely, and who holds standing privilege? Proactive, continuous hygiene across human + workload identities — not runtime alerting.

02
What it inspects — the recurring classes of identity risk
🔑
MFA gaps & legacy auth — protocols allowed without MFA.
💤
Dormant / orphaned accounts — stale identities nobody uses.
🕵️
Shadow admins — over-privileged roles & hidden escalation.
🔌
Risky OAuth grants — third-party apps with wide scopes.
🎚️
Misconfigured conditional access — gaps in the policy set.
🏰
AD misconfigs — risky ACLs, unconstrained delegation.

Plus excessive standing privilege and poor credential hygiene — the raw material of an identity attack.

03
How it works — from IdPs & AD, to prioritized identity-risk findings

Connect → inventory identities & config → evaluate vs best practice → prioritize by exposure → guide the fix. A continuous loop, not a one-time audit.

04
Standing privilege

The prize an attacker hunts — access that is always on, whether or not it's in use:

👑
Excess admin roles — more privilege than any job needs.
🕵️
Shadow admins — effective admin via nested groups & ACLs.
💤
Dormant privilege — powerful accounts nobody watches.

Least privilege — for the identity fabric itself.

05
An identity attack path

ISPM breaks the chain early — close the MFA gap before the escalation ever runs.

06
ISPM vs ITDR vs CIEM vs PAM — four identity tools that stack together

🧭 ISPM

Identity config / hygiene posture — is the fabric set up safely?

🚨 ITDR

Identity threat detection — runtime detection of identity attacks.

☁️ CIEM

Cloud entitlements — right-sizing permissions in the cloud.

🔐 PAM

Privileged-access control — vaulting & brokering admin access.

ISPM = posture · ITDR = runtime detection · CIEM = cloud entitlements · PAM = access control. They complement, not replace, each other.

07

You can't do least-privilege if you can't see who holds standing privilege.

09
Watch out
🌉
Hybrid coverage — on-prem AD and cloud IdPs both need eyes; a gap on either side is the gap.
🚫
Posture ≠ runtime detection — ISPM hardens config; ITDR catches the live attack.
🔓
Remediate without lockouts — pulling privilege too fast breaks people; stage the fix.
🤖
Workload identities too — service accounts & tokens sprawl faster than humans do.
