01
What it is

The Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard — one baseline for protecting cardholder data, run by the PCI Security Standards Council (founded by the major card brands).

Contractual, not statutory — you accept it by accepting cards. It applies to anyone who stores, processes, or transmits that data.

02
Who must comply
🗄️
Store — you keep card numbers anywhere, in any form.
⚙️
Process — your systems handle the data during a payment.
📡
Transmit — card data crosses your network or app.

Touch cardholder data any of these ways → you're in scope.

03
6 goals → 12 requirements — the whole standard, grouped

Six goals organise the twelve requirements — a layered defence from the network edge (1–2) to the people and policies that run it (12).

04
The Cardholder Data Environment — shrink the CDE, shrink your audit

Scope = the CDE: every system that touches card data. Cut it with tokenization, network segmentation, and outsourcing to compliant processors — a token isn't card data, so systems that hold only tokens fall out of scope.

05
What v4.x changed
🔁
Continuous, not annual — security as an ongoing process, not a once-a-year snapshot.
🧩
Customized approach — meet the outcome your own way, beside the defined controls.
🔐
Stronger auth — MFA required for all access into the CDE.
🛒
Anti-skimming — new client-side script & e-commerce protections.
06
Two ways to comply
ApproachHow you meet it
DefinedFollow the stated control, literally.
CustomizedMeet the objective your own way

Customized suits mature teams — but demands a targeted risk analysis to prove the outcome is met.

07
Merchant levels 1–4

Validation scales by yearly transaction volume:

Level 1

Largest volume → full ROC by a QSA + on-site audit.

Level 2–3

Mid volume → an SAQ, sometimes with a QSA.

Level 4

Smallest → self-assessment SAQ.

08
How you validate
📝
SAQ — a Self-Assessment Questionnaire you attest to.
🔍
QSA · ROC — a Qualified Security Assessor issues a Report on Compliance.
📡
ASV scan — quarterly external vuln scans by an Approved Scanning Vendor.
09
The v4 timeline — the phase-in is complete

v4.0.1 added & removed no requirements — it clarified v4.0. As of 31 Mar 2025, the 51 future-dated new requirements are in full force.

10
Watch out — where compliant orgs still get breached
📐
Scope sprawl — flat networks pull everything into the CDE. Segment hard.
📅
"Compliant once a year" — controls drift the day after the audit. v4 wants continuous.
🛒
E-commerce skimming — injected client-side scripts (Magecart) steal cards in the browser.
⚖️
Compliant ≠ secure — it's a floor, not a ceiling. Attackers don't read the SAQ.
11
The takeaway
PCI DSS is a contractual baseline for card data — 12 requirements over the CDE, best met by shrinking scope and staying continuously secure, not passing one yearly audit.
Scope the CDE→ Tokenize · segment→ Meet the 12→ Validate & sustain
