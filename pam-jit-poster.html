PRIVILEGED ACCESS

Kill the always-on admin keys — grant power just-in-time, for a reason, then take it back automatically (PAM · JIT · Zero Standing Privileges)
CTF Farooq Ali
· security explainer series
v2026.06
01
The problem — standing admin is an always-on attack surface

Standing privileges are always-on keys — a permanent surface attackers hunt for. Take the key off the ring until it's actually needed.

02
PAM — privileged access management

Put every privileged account behind a controlled front door:

🗄️
Vault + broker — credentials live in a vault; the broker checks them out, nobody sees the raw secret.
🔄
Rotate secrets — passwords & keys are changed automatically, often after each use.
🔐
MFA to elevate — strong auth required before any privileged session starts.
🎥
Record the session — privileged sessions are proxied, logged & replayable.
03
JIT — just-in-time access

Grant admin only when needed, for a stated reason, for a limited time.

request→ approve→ elevate→ auto-expire

The grant carries a who, a why, and a when-until — a short TTL, not an open-ended role.

Access has a start, a reason, and an end — not a permanent grant.

04
Zero Standing Privileges — the goal

No permanent admin at all. At rest, every account sits at zero privilege.

0️⃣
Baseline = nothing — no standing role, no idle key to steal.
⏱️
Ephemeral privilege — created on demand, revoked the moment the task is done.

ZSP is the destination; JIT + PAM are how you get there.

05
The model — a request → elevate → auto-revoke cycle, back to zero

The privilege exists only inside the loop — outside it, there is nothing to attack.

06
Why it works
💥
Smaller blast radius — a compromised account is powerless until (and unless) it elevates.
🔑
Fewer standing credentials — far less to steal, phish, or leave lying around.
📋
Every elevation is logged — who, when, why, for how long — auditable by default.
07
The pieces — what you assemble
vaultsession broker / proxyapproval workflowsecrets rotationaudit & session recording

Vault holds it · broker brokers it · workflow gates it · rotation refreshes it · audit remembers it.

08

09
Watch out
🧯
Break-glass accounts — emergency admins are standing privilege by design. Vault, alert on use, review after.
🗝️
Over-broad roles — JIT to a role that's still god-mode barely helps. Scope the grant, not just the timing.
🥱
Approval fatigue — floods of requests get rubber-stamped. Tune thresholds; auto-approve low-risk.
🤖
Non-human identities — service accounts & machine creds are the ones most often left standing.
10
A grant — what one request looks like
// user asks the broker for a time-boxed elevation
POST /elevate   role="db-admin" & reason="INC-4821 hotfix" & ttl=30m
// ← approved → an ephemeral, expiring grant
{ "granted": "db-admin", "expires_at": "14:30Z", "revoke": "auto" }

No reason, no grant. No TTL, no grant. The clock starts the moment it's issued.

11
The takeaway
No standing privilege — grant admin just-in-time, just-enough, and take it back automatically.
Zero at rest→ Request + reason→ Time-boxed elevate→ Auto-revoke
