01
What it is

A knowledge graph of defensive cybersecurity countermeasures — the defensive counterpart to ATT&CK. Developed by MITRE, funded by the NSA.

Not a product to buy — a precise, shared vocabulary for the defenses that counter attacker behavior.

02
Why it exists
⚔️
ATT&CK names the offense — a catalog of how attackers behave, step by step.
🛡️
D3FEND names the defense — a precise vocabulary for the countermeasures that answer it.

Two teams, one language — so defenders can talk about controls as rigorously as attackers talk about techniques.

03
The defensive tactics — the seven categories techniques are organized under

Model · Harden · Detect · Isolate · Deceive · Evict · Restore — every defensive technique lives under one of these.

04
The Digital Artifact Ontology — the glue that links offense to defense

The artifact is the join key. Both an attack and a defense reference the same digital objects — so D3FEND can link a technique to the countermeasures that touch the same artifact.

05
ATT&CK ↔ D3FEND mapping — pick a technique · surface the countermeasures

One attacker move fans out to many defenses — D3FEND shows which countermeasures harden against, detect, or evict it.

06
How to use it — coverage analysis: map your controls, expose your gaps

"What can we DETECT? what can we STOP? what can we CONTAIN?" — the map turns a wall of techniques into a punch list.

08
It complements
ATT&CK · offense+ D3FEND · defense

The two halves of one picture. D3FEND doesn't replace your tools — it gives them a frame.

Pair it with detection engineering + your SIEM.

09
Watch out
🛒
It's not a product — a vocabulary + knowledge graph, not a control you buy or deploy.
🧭
A mapping ≠ a control — knowing the countermeasure isn't the same as running it.
🏷️
It's versioned — still maturing; cite the version and check the current spec.
📐
Coverage ≠ security — it exposes gaps, but you still have to close them.
10
The takeaway
D3FEND is the defender's map — for every attacker move in ATT&CK, the countermeasures that harden, detect, isolate, and evict.
Pick a technique→ Surface countermeasures→ Map coverage→ Close the gaps
