01
What it is

A community project by the Open Worldwide Application Security Project — a ranked list of the ten most critical web-app risk categories, refreshed from real-world data + a practitioner survey.

Awareness first — it names the classes of failure every builder should design against.

02
How it's ranked
📊
Data-driven — eight categories from measured incidence across hundreds of thousands of apps.
🗳️
Survey-driven — two from a practitioner survey, to catch what tooling can't yet measure.

Categories, not single bugs — each groups many related weaknesses (CWEs).

04
What changed since 2021 — two new categories, one consolidated in

Two brand-new categories — A03 Software Supply Chain Failures (expanding 2021's "Vulnerable & Outdated Components") and A10 Mishandling of Exceptional Conditions. 2021's SSRF folded into A01.

05
Spotlight · A01 stays #1

Broken Access Control is the most common serious risk — an authenticated user reaching data or actions that aren't theirs.

force a URL / ID→ no check→ someone else's data

Deny by default; enforce authz server-side on every request.

06
Spotlight · A03 supply chain

Your app is only as trustworthy as its dependencies, build & pipeline. A poisoned package or CI step ships straight to prod.

pin versionsverify signaturesSBOMlock the pipeline

Know — and vet — everything you didn't write.

07
Spotlight · A10 exceptions

When something goes wrong, code can fail open, leak internals, or crash into an insecure state.

🔓
Fail open — an error skips the auth check instead of denying.
🗒️
Leaky errors — stack traces & secrets shown to the user.

Fail closed; return a clean, generic error.

08
How to use the list — awareness & threat modeling, not a tick-box audit

It's a starting map, not a finish line — "no Top 10 findings" ≠ secure. Pair it with the OWASP ASVS.

The OWASP Top 10:2025 is a shared map of what breaks — the ten risk classes every web-app builder should design against, not just scan for.
