GDPR — DATA PROTECTION

The EU's privacy law for personal data — rights for people, duties for anyone who handles their data (Regulation 2016/679; in force 25 May 2018 — verify specifics against the current text)

01
What it is

The EU's General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation 2016/679) — in force since 25 May 2018. It protects the personal data of people in the EU/EEA, giving individuals rights and placing duties on whoever collects or uses that data.

Privacy is the default — you must justify collecting and using personal data.

02
Who it reaches
🌍
Extraterritorial — applies to any organization worldwide that targets or monitors people in the EU.
🛒
The trigger is the person — offering goods/services to, or tracking, EU individuals.

"We're a US company, so it doesn't apply" is a myth — location of the person, not the firm, decides.

03
The roles & the data flow — controller decides, processor acts, rights flow back to the person

A DPO (Data Protection Officer) is required in some cases; national supervisory authorities (DPAs) enforce it.

04
The 7 principles — Art. 5
lawful · fair · transparentpurpose limitationdata minimisationaccuracystorage limitationintegrity & confidentialityaccountability

Accountability means you must be able to prove you meet the other six.

05
The 6 lawful bases — Art. 6

You need one valid basis for every processing activity:

consent· contract· legal obligation· vital interests· public task· legitimate interests

Consent is only one of six — often not the best fit.

06
Data subject rights — what the individual can demand

ℹ️ Be informed · access

Know that you're processing their data — and get a copy of it.

✏️ Rectify · erase

Fix errors; delete it — the "right to be forgotten".

⛔ Restrict · object

Pause processing, or object to it — including direct marketing.

📦 Portability

Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable form.

🤖 Automated decisions

Rights against decisions made solely by automation / profiling.

⏱️ Respond in time

Usually within one month, and free of charge.

07
Core duties
🛡️
Privacy by design & default — build data protection in from the start.
📒
Records of processing — maintain a register of your processing activities.
🔍
DPIA — assess high-risk processing before you start it.
✈️
Lawful transfers — adequacy decisions or Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs).
08
The fines

The top tier for the most serious breaches — whichever figure is higher:

A lower band (€10M / 2%) applies to less severe breaches.

09
Breach notice — tell the DPA within 72 hours of becoming aware
10
GDPR ≠ just security — security (Art. 32) is one piece of a broader privacy law

Locking data down satisfies Art. 32 — but GDPR also governs why and how you use it.

11
Watch out
🇺🇸
"US company, doesn't apply" — it's the person's location that triggers GDPR.
☑️
Consent as a default — it's one of six bases, and easily withdrawn.
✈️
Careless transfers — sending data abroad needs adequacy or SCCs.
12
The takeaway
GDPR gives people rights over their personal data and holds you accountable for a lawful basis, real security, and honouring those rights — not just locking data down.
Know your data→ Pick a lawful basis→ Honour the rights→ Prove it
