01
What GRC is — Governance · Risk · Compliance

🧭 Governance

Sets direction — the policies, roles, and accountability that decide how security is run and who owns it.

⚖️ Risk

Decides what to protect and how much to spend — identify, rate, and treat risk (accept · mitigate · transfer · avoid).

📋 Compliance

Proves it — meets external rules and shows evidence to auditors, customers, and regulators.

Govern says what matters · Risk decides where to invest · Compliance demonstrates you did.

02
Compliance ≠ Security — the one idea to carry into every other poster

Passing an audit means you met a baseline, on paper, at a moment in time — attackers do not care about your certificate.

Aim for security; treat compliance as the byproduct. Audit-ready ≠ attack-ready.

03
The landscape sorts into three buckets — by how binding each one is

📜 Laws / Regulations

Mandatory — fines if you don't. GDPR · HIPAA · PCI DSS (contractual-mandatory)

📐 Frameworks

Voluntary guidance — a best-practice map you choose to follow. NIST CSF · CIS Controls · ISO 27001 · CISA ZTMM

🏅 Attestations / Certs

Proof you show others — an audited result. SOC 2 report · ISO 27001 cert · FedRAMP ATO · CMMC

Note: ISO 27001 is both a framework (the standard) and a certification (the audited badge) — the same name plays two roles.

05
Controls are the shared currency — map once, comply many (a “crosswalk”)

One control satisfies many frameworks. Implement it once, map it across — control catalogs (NIST 800-53, CIS) underpin the higher-level compliance frameworks.

06
Prescriptive vs outcome-based
📏
Prescriptive — tells you specifically what to do. CIS Controls · PCI DSS
🎯
Outcome-based — states a goal, you choose how. NIST CSF · ISO 27001

Prescriptive is easier to audit; outcome-based fits more contexts.

10
Watch out
🎭
Audit theater — tidying evidence for the auditor while real risk goes untreated.
☑️
Checkbox security — doing the minimum to pass, not what actually protects.
📄
Certificate ≠ secure — a badge is a snapshot in time, not a live guarantee.
11
The takeaway
GRC = Govern · Risk · Comply — but compliance is the byproduct of real security, never a substitute for it.
Govern (direction)→ Risk (what to protect)→ Controls (map once)→ Comply (prove it)
