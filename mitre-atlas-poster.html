01
What ATLAS is — an ATT&CK-style map of attacks on AI

ATLAS = Adversarial Threat Landscape for Artificial-Intelligence Systems — a living, ATT&CK-style knowledge base of real-world attacks on AI/ML systems, curated by MITRE.

🗺️ Tactics & techniques

The attacker's goals (tactics) and the ways they reach them (techniques) — the ATT&CK grammar, applied to AI.

📚 Grounded in incidents

Not theory — built from documented case studies of attacks seen in the wild.

🔁 Living matrix

Expands fast as new GenAI & agent attacks emerge — a moving target, by design.

02
ATT&CK vs ATLAS — same model, extended to the AI attack surface

ATLAS reuses ATT&CK's tactic/technique model and adds AI-native tactics — like ML Model Access and ML Attack Staging.

03
The tactics — the attacker's goals, left → right across the matrix

Full set mirrors ATT&CK + AI-native tactics: Reconnaissance · Resource Development · Initial Access · ML Model Access · Execution · Persistence · Privilege Escalation · Defense Evasion · Credential Access · Discovery · Collection · ML Attack Staging · Exfiltration · Impact.

04
Signature AI techniques
☣️
Data Poisoning — corrupt training data to plant a backdoor.
🥸
Model Evasion — adversarial examples fool the model at inference.
🪞
Model Extraction — query the API to steal / clone the model.
🔎
Inversion & Membership Inference — recover training data.
💬
Prompt Injection — untrusted text hijacks an LLM.
📦
ML Supply-Chain Compromise — tainted model / dataset.
05
The AI attack surface

One target, five ways in — model, data, pipeline, prompts & agents.

06
The GenAI + agent expansion — what ATLAS grew to cover
📥
RAG Poisoning — malicious docs seeded into the knowledge base.
🕳️
False RAG Entry Injection — fake retrieved "facts" steer the answer.
🧩
LLM Prompt Crafting — tuned prompts to jailbreak or coax the model.
🎭
Impersonation — pose as a trusted user, tool, or service.
📦
AI Supply-Chain Compromise — tainted model, adapter, or plugin.
🤝
Multi-agent trust exploitation — one hijacked agent misleads the rest.

Agents don't just add tools — they add new ways to be lied to and hijacked.

10
Watch out — what AI attacks actually target
🧠
The model itself — evasion & extraction hit the weights, not the app.
☣️
The training data — poisoning plants backdoors long before deploy.
🔧
The ML pipeline — supply-chain compromise taints models & datasets.
🤖
Then agents — tools, RAG & multi-agent trust widen the surface further.

Securing the surrounding app is necessary but not sufficient — the AI is its own attack surface.
