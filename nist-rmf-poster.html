01
What it is

The NIST Risk Management Framework (SP 800-37 Rev 2) — the repeatable, risk-based process US federal agencies use to secure and authorize an information system to operate. Mandated under FISMA.

The "how" — the disciplined path a system walks to earn an Authorization to Operate (ATO).

02
Why it exists
📏
Risk, decided consistently — one repeatable process so every system is assessed and authorized the same way.
🔁
Risk is continuous — systems and threats drift, so authorization is ongoing, never a one-time sign-off.

Built on the NIST catalog — FIPS 199/200, SP 800-53 & 53A, SP 800-60.

03
The seven steps — Prepare once, then the loop that never stops

Prepare gets the org and system ready; then Categorize → Select → Implement → Assess → Authorize → Monitor — and monitoring loops back in whenever the system or its risk changes.

04
Step 1 · Categorize — rate the system's impact with FIPS 199

Rate the worst-case impact on Confidentiality, Integrity & Availability as Low / Moderate / High. The system's category is the high-water mark across all three — and it drives everything downstream.

05
Step 2 · Select

The category picks a starting SP 800-53 control baseline — then you tailor it to the real system:

LOW baseline· MOD· HIGH→ tailor

FIPS 200 sets the minimum; SP 800-53 is the catalog you pull from and tune.

06
Steps 3–4 · Implement & Assess
🔧
Implement — deploy the selected controls and write down how each is met.
🧪
Assess — an assessor tests the controls per SP 800-53A; findings go in a plan of action.

Assessment produces the evidence the authorizing official will weigh.

07
Step 5 · Authorize — one accountable human accepts the residual risk

The Authorizing Official — not the security team — decides whether the leftover (residual) risk is acceptable. If yes, they grant an ATO: a time-boxed, accountable go-live decision they personally own.

08
Step 6 · Monitor — continuous, not a checkbox

An ATO isn't the finish line. ConMon keeps the risk picture current so authorization stays valid:

control statusconfig & changevuln scansre-assessongoing authorization

Findings loop back — a big enough change re-triggers categorize, assess, and re-authorize.

10

11
Watch out
📄
ATO as paperwork — a binder to file, not a real risk-acceptance decision. The classic failure.
😴
Weak ConMon — an ATO with stale monitoring is a rubber stamp; the risk picture goes dark the day it's signed.
🙈
Under-categorizing — low-balling FIPS 199 to dodge controls just pushes real risk downstream.
🧾
Controls on paper — "documented" isn't "tested"; the SP 800-53A assessment is what proves it.
12
The takeaway
The NIST RMF is the mandatory federal process to reach an ATO — categorize the risk, select & test controls, let an Authorizing Official accept the residual risk, then monitor continuously.
Categorize→ Select→ Implement→ Assess→ Authorize (ATO)→ Monitor↺
