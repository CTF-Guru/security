CLOUD DETECTION & RESPONSE

CDR — real-time threat detection & response for the cloud, the cloud-native analog of EDR / XDR. When prevention fails, it catches the attack in progress (posture asks “is it safe?”; CDR asks “is it under attack right now?”)

v2026.06
01
What CDR is — EDR / XDR, but for the cloud

Cloud Detection & Response watches cloud telemetry — control plane & workloads — for active attacks, then investigates and responds. The detect-and-respond job of EDR/XDR, aimed at the cloud.

02
What it watches — cloud telemetry

It ingests the signals that reveal what's happening in the cloud, right now:

📜
Control-plane logs — AWS CloudTrail, Azure & GCP audit logs: every API call.
🌐
Network flow — VPC flow logs, DNS & egress traffic.
☸️
Kubernetes audit — API-server events, orchestration activity.
⚙️
Workload / runtime — process, file & syscall signals on hosts & containers.
03
Threats it catches — active, not hypothetical

Behaviour that means an attack is underway, not just a weak config:

🔑
Credential abuse — stolen keys, anomalous API calls from new geos.
👑
Privilege escalation — rights being granted & assumed mid-attack.
📤
Data exfiltration — mass reads, snapshots copied out, egress spikes.
⛏️
Crypto-mining · lateral movement — hijacked compute, host-to-host spread.
04
The loop — detect → investigate → respond, on live cloud telemetry

Ingest → detect → investigate → respond — a continuous runtime loop that shrinks how long an attacker goes unnoticed.

05
Posture vs detection — why CDR completes a CNAPP
CSPM · CWPP · CIEMCDR
JobPosture — hygiene & configDetection & response
StanceProactive — preventReactive — catch it live
WhenBuild & config timeRuntime, as it happens
Answers“Is it configured safely?”“Is something attacking now?”

Posture reduces the chance of a breach; CDR is what you have for when prevention fails. Together they make a complete CNAPP.

06
A cloud attack, in minutes — where CDR earns its keep

Cloud attacks unfold in minutes, not days. CDR detects & responds mid-chain — cutting dwell time before the data leaves.

07
CDR vs its siblings — say it clearly
🎯
CDR — active threats now. Runtime detect + respond.
📐
CSPM — is the config safe? Posture.
🛡️
CWPP — are the workloads hardened? Posture.
🪪
CIEM — who can do what? Posture.

CSPM / CWPP / CIEM harden the cloud; CDR watches it while it runs.

08
Ties to the SOC — SIEM · SOAR · XDR

CDR is the cloud-focused member of the detect-&-respond family:

🗄️
SIEM — CDR can feed cloud detections into the central SOC pipeline.
🤖
SOAR — its response playbooks drive automated containment.
🧩
XDR — CDR is the cloud layer; XDR correlates it with endpoint & network.

CDR complements the SOC stack with a cloud focus — it doesn't replace it.

09
Why it exists — shrink cloud dwell time (MTTD → MTTR)
prevention fails→ CDR detects fast (MTTD↓)→ investigate the path→ respond fast (MTTR↓)

Posture lowers the odds; CDR lowers the damage. The two metrics that matter — MTTD (detect) & MTTR (respond) — CDR pulls both down.

10
Watch out
🕳️
Log coverage & cost — blind spots miss attacks; full telemetry can get expensive.
⏱️
Cloud-speed attacks — minutes, not days. Detection must be near real-time.
🔔
Noisy telemetry — raw logs are loud; good detections beat more alerts.
11
Auto-response, carefully

Automated response is powerful — and dangerous without guardrails:

✂️
Auto-revoke can break prod — killing a “rogue” key may kill a real service.
🚦
Scope the playbooks — gate risky actions; alert-only in sensitive paths.

Fast response is the point — but a wrong auto-action is its own outage.

12
The takeaway
CDR is EDR/XDR for the cloud — it watches cloud telemetry to catch an attack in progress. Posture prevents; CDR is for when prevention fails.
Ingest telemetry→ Detect the attack→ Investigate the path→ Respond — shrink dwell time
