01
What it is

Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification — a US Department of Defense program that verifies that companies in the Defense Industrial Base (the DIB) adequately protect the government data they touch.

It turns "trust me, we're secure" into checked, certified, contract-enforced security.

02
What it protects
📄
FCI — Federal Contract Information: not-public info provided or generated under a contract. Baseline safeguarding.
🔐
CUI — Controlled Unclassified Info: sensitive gov data (tech specs, drawings) needing far stronger controls.

Which data you handle sets your level — FCI alone vs CUI is the dividing line.

03
The three levels — higher data sensitivity → more controls · stricter assessment

The level is set by the data and the program — FCI only → L1, CUI → L2, and the most critical CUI programs → L3.

04

05
Built on NIST

Level 2 is NIST SP 800-171 — CMMC verifies it, it doesn't reinvent it. The lineage:

NIST RMF · 800-53→ SP 800-171→ CMMC L2

If you already meet 800-171, you're most of the way to L2.

06
It flows downhill
⬇️
Flow-down — prime contractors push CMMC requirements down to their subcontractors.
✍️
Annual affirmation — a senior company official must formally affirm continued compliance each year.

One weak sub can block the whole award — the bar reaches your suppliers too.

07
The phased rollout — 32 CFR program rule + 48 CFR contract clause · mid-2026 = you are here

Self-assessments appear first (Phase 1), then L2 third-party certification becomes mandatory (Phase 2) — plan ahead of your contract's phase.

08
POA&Ms are limited

You can't defer everything with a "we'll fix it later" plan (a POA&M):

🎯
Many controls must be fully met at assessment — no POA&M allowed.
⏳
Time-boxed — allowed gaps must be closed within a fixed window (≈180 days).
09
Watch out
🔍
Scoping CUI — mis-drawing the boundary of where CUI lives is the #1 pitfall.
📅
Assessment lead times — C3PAO capacity is finite; book early or miss the bid.
🧾
Self ≠ done — a self-assessment still needs real evidence and a senior affirmation.
10
Why it exists

Adversaries target the weakest supplier, not the prime. Self-attestation left gaps; CMMC makes the same 800-171 controls auditable and enforceable before award.

No certification, no contract — security becomes a gate, not a promise.

11
The takeaway
CMMC 2.0 makes DIB security verified, not self-declared — match your level to your data, meet NIST 800-171, and get assessed before your contract's phase lands.
Scope FCI / CUI→ Pick the level→ Meet 800-171→ Self or C3PAO assess→ Affirm annually
