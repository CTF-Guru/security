01
What the ZTMM is — a self-assessment roadmap, not a product or certification
🗺️
A roadmap — CISA's guide for US federal agencies to plan & measure their journey to zero trust, in support of OMB Memo M-22-09.
📐
Grounded in NIST — it operationalises NIST SP 800-207 (Zero Trust Architecture) into pillars and measurable stages.
📊
A maturity model — you locate where you are per pillar, then plan the path forward. Version 2.0, April 2023.

Not a box you buy and not a badge you earn — a shared yardstick for progress toward zero trust.

Five pillars on three cross-cutting foundations — the shape of the model

Five pillars stand on three foundations — the capabilities that span every pillar.

03
What secures what — five pillars, plus three capabilities that cut across them
🪪
Identity — strong auth & MFA; who is making the request.
💻
Devices — inventory & health of every endpoint.
🌐
Networks — segment, encrypt, and control traffic.
📦
Apps & Workloads — secure delivery of every app.
🗄️
Data — classify, tag, and protect it end-to-end.

The three aren't pillars — they run through all five, maturing alongside them:

🔎
Visibility & Analytics — see everything; feed decisions with data.
⚙️
Automation & Orchestration — respond at machine speed, not by hand.
📋
Governance — policy, roles & oversight tie it together.
04
Four maturity stages — a ladder you climb, assessed per pillar

Traditional → Initial → Advanced → Optimal — each pillar climbs on its own, at its own pace.

05

fully automated continuous least-privilege policy-driven dynamic

07
What it's built on

📜 OMB M-22-09

The 2022 federal mandate to move agencies to a zero-trust architecture.

📐 NIST 800-207

The Zero Trust Architecture standard the ZTMM operationalises.

🏛️ CISA ZTMM

Turns those into pillars & stages agencies can measure against.

Mandate → architecture → measurable roadmap. Written for US federal agencies — but the 5 pillars & 4 stages are vendor-neutral and free for anyone to adopt.

08
How to use it

Self-assess each pillar, then plan toward Optimal:

1 · locate the stage→ 2 · set a target→ 3 · close gaps→ 4 · re-assess

Not Optimal everywhere at once — advance the highest-risk pillars first, then iterate.

09
ZTMM vs ZTNA vs Zero Trust — three things people conflate

Zero Trust

The concept — "never trust, always verify." Verify explicitly · least privilege · assume breach.the mindset

ZTNA

The access model — grant one app, never the network. Identity-aware, default-deny.a technology

ZTMM

The cross-pillar maturity roadmap — where are you, and how do you advance?the yardstick

The concept sets the goal · ZTNA is one way to reach it · the ZTMM measures the journey.

10
Watch out
⚖️
Uneven pillars — an Optimal Identity sitting next to a Traditional Data pillar still leaves you exposed. Balance the climb across all five.
🛒
Buying "a zero trust box" — no single product makes you mature. It's an architecture and a program, not a purchase.
🐢
A journey, not a project — maturity is continuous; there's no "done," only the next stage. Advance the highest-risk pillars first.
11
The takeaway
The ZTMM turns zero trust from a slogan into a measurable roadmap — five pillars, four stages, climbed toward Optimal.
Assess each pillar→ Traditional → Optimal→ Balance the climb→ It's a journey
